What Does "Ex-UK" Mean?

Ex-UK laptops are machines that were originally purchased and used in the United Kingdom, then exported to Kenya for resale. They are not brand new, but they are typically in very good to excellent condition — clean, functional, and significantly cheaper than new equivalents. Most are corporate machines decommissioned after 2-3 years of use.

We inspect every ex-UK unit carefully. If something is not right, we fix it before putting it on sale. You always know what you are getting at Nexcom.

Refurbished Laptop FAQs

Is it safe to buy a refurbished laptop?

Yes — when bought from a trusted seller. At Nexcom, every refurbished laptop is tested before sale. We check the battery, screen, keyboard, and internal components. We also provide a short warranty so you are covered if anything goes wrong.

What is the difference between refurbished and ex-UK?

Both mean second-hand, but ex-UK specifically refers to laptops previously used in the UK. Refurbished may include local trade-ins we have repaired and cleaned up. Both categories are tested and ready to use.
